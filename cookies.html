Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and similar tracking technologies

Last Updated: February 2024

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit our website. They help us recognize you, remember your preferences, and improve your experience. Cookies also allow us to track your use of our site for analytics and marketing purposes.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas. Without these cookies, the website cannot function effectively.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are most popular and how visitors navigate the site. This helps us improve the performance and user experience of our services.

Functional Cookies

These cookies remember choices you make (such as your language preference or username) to provide personalized features and functionality.

Marketing and Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to track your activity across websites and deliver personalized advertisements. They help us understand your interests and show you relevant content.

3. How Long Do Cookies Last?

Cookies may be either "session cookies" (temporary, deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (remain on your device until they expire or you delete them). The duration varies depending on the type and purpose of the cookie.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may allow third-party service providers to place cookies on your device for analytics, advertising, and other purposes. These include:

  • Google Analytics (for website traffic analysis)
  • Facebook Pixel (for marketing and conversion tracking)
  • Payment processors (for transaction handling)
  • Other marketing partners

5. Your Cookie Choices

You have the right to accept or refuse cookies. Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject all cookies (may affect functionality)
  • Accept only essential cookies
  • Delete cookies from your browser
  • Set preferences for specific cookie types

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for recognizing DNT signals. College Charter does not currently respond to DNT browser signals, but we provide you with detailed choices about cookies as described in this policy.

7. Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, we ask for your consent to use non-essential cookies. You can change your cookie preferences at any time through your account settings.

8. Impact of Refusing Cookies

If you refuse non-essential cookies, our website will still function, but some features may not work optimally. You will see a limited personalized experience and may not receive targeted content.

9. Security

We take reasonable measures to secure cookies and prevent unauthorized access. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is completely secure.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.
